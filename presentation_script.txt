Slide 2:

"So, what exactly is Artificial Intelligence? Simply put, AI is a way to teach computers to do things that normally require human brainpower."
"Normally, computers only do exactly what we code them to do, step-by-step. But with AI, we can train computers to recognize faces, understand human speech, and even beat world champions at chess or video games!"

Slide 3:

"But how do they learn this? Through a process called Machine Learning."
"Think about how a small baby learns what a 'dog' is. You point to a dog and say 'dog.' You do this a dozen times. Eventually, their brain picks up the pattern: four legs, fur, tail, barks. AI works the exact same way!"
"We feed the computer thousands of pictures of dogs. These pictures are called Data. The AI looks at the data, finds the patterns, and eventually learns to recognize a dog on its own."

Slide 4:

"One of the coolest types of AI right now is called Generative AI. Instead of just sorting data (like recognizing a dog), it actually creates new things—like drawing art, making music, or writing text."
"The most famous one right now is ChatGPT. Think of ChatGPT as a massive, super-powered autocomplete. It has read almost the entire internet, so when you ask it a question, it predicts the absolute best words to respond with."
Interactive Demo: "Let's test it live. I need your help to give ChatGPT a prompt. Type in the chat or shout out: an Animal, a Location, and an Object!"

"Generative AI isn't just for writing funny stories. It can also act as an incredible personal tutor. Let's look at how AI approaches math."
"Math is basically the ultimate treasure hunt—and our goal is always to find 'x'. Here we have the equation: 3x plus 5 equals 17."

Slide 7:

"Those demos were awesome! But here is the catch we will talk about a lot this week: AI is only as smart as the data we give it."
"Imagine we train an AI to recognize apples, but we only show it pictures of red apples. If we hand it a green apple, it will fail. It won't know what it's looking at. This is called Bias."
"If we aren't careful, the AI we build can reflect our own blind spots and make unfair decisions. It is up to us—the next generation of AI Innovators in this room—to make sure we build AI that is fair, safe, and helpful to everyone."

Slide 8:

"Because AI isn't just about chatting or making recipes—it's out there in the real world saving lives and making things easier. When you see a self-driving car, that's Computer Vision in action—the AI is 'seeing' and identifying stop signs and pedestrians in real-time."
"In hospitals, AI helps doctors analyze X-rays faster than humans ever could to catch illnesses early. And on a daily basis, when Spotify knows exactly which song you want to hear next, that's a Recommender System studying your patterns to personalize your world."
